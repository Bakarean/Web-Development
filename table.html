  • Metaphor(Implied resemblances):

    Derived from human physiology are commonly extented to nature or inanimate objects as in the expressions "the mouth of a river", "the bowels of earth",or "the eye of the needle".

  • Simile(A comparison indicated by "like" or "as"):

    Is exemplified in "we were packed in the room like sardines" or "he is as slow as molasses".

  • Metonomy(Using the name of one thing for another closely related to it)

    "The power of the crown was mortally weakned", where "crown" means "king" or "queen".

  • Synecdoche()use of a part to inply the whole

    In expressions such as "brass" for high ranking military officers or "hard hats" for contruction woekers

  • Hyperbole(Deliberate exaggeration for the sake of effect)

    as in "i'm so mad i could chew nails".

  • Rhetorical-question(Asked for effect, with no answer expected)

    As in "how can i express my thanks to you?"

  • Litotes(concious understatement in which enphasis is achieved by negation)

    As in "it's no fun to be sick "
